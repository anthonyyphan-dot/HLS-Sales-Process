Step 01
Introduction
What to cover on every opening call
  • Introduce yourself — your name, company, and why you're calling. Provide call recording advice.
  • Understand the what & the why — uncover overarching goals (both long-term and short-term).
  • Deliver your value proposition — how do Cribs' services benefit this customer? What sets us apart from banks and other brokers?
  • Set the agenda — tell the customer what will happen during this call and what the outcome will be.

"I'm busy / in a meeting right now"
Your response Completely understand, I've called you in the middle of a workday. I wasn't looking to take too much of your time right now — I just wanted to follow up on the research you were doing on our platform. I can call you back at another time, but I just wanted to make sure we could help you out. Were you looking at refinancing or purchasing a property?
"I've already got another mortgage broker"
Your response That's great to hear. I'm not looking to step on anyone's toes — what we can do is provide a few additional options from our panel of lenders so you can make sure you're proceeding with the right choice. What has your broker offered you so far?
"I think I'm all good for now"
Your response That's all good — I just wanted to reach out since you came onto our platform recently. Just to double-check: what rate are you sitting on at the moment? I can quickly tell you if we can do something better.
"I was just doing research / checking borrowing power"
Your response That's all good — glad you were able to use our platform. I just wanted to reach out and see if I could assist with anything. When were you thinking about potentially purchasing a property?

Step 03
Providing a Solution & Product Comparison
Comparison process — in order
  1. Complete the Needs & Objectives tabs before building the comparison — you must understand the customer's preferences first.
  2. Always send a minimum of 3 products. The first product must be the cheapest rate, even if it's not your preferred recommendation.
  3. Send the link and wait for the customer to open it themselves. Guide them through the login process.
  4. As you walk through each lender, continuously ask: "What rate can you see?" or "Can you see the repayment?" — this keeps the customer engaged and following along.
  5. After reviewing all products, make a clear recommendation and explain why the other two options are not the best fit for this customer.

Step 04
6 Steps to Close
  • 1
    Pick a Bank
    After your recommendation, ask the customer which lender they prefer. Ensure they tentatively select one — even if they raise an objection. Use AAPA if needed.
  • 2
    Sell Intellectually
    Re-explain the savings clearly. Use the savings calculator where applicable. For purchases, show how extra repayments or an offset/redraw facility accelerates loan payoff.
  • 3
    Sell Emotionally
    Link back to the customer's "why" and build genuine urgency and excitement. Example: "The sooner we get you pre-approved, the sooner you can make an offer and stop paying someone else's mortgage."
  • 4
    Gain a Commitment
    "Just to confirm — you're happy to proceed with XYZ Bank as presented. If you change your mind, we can always switch to another lender."
  • 5
    VVDR
    Walk through the four pillars needed to progress the application.
    VOI Valuation Documents Referrals
  • 6
    Close with an Agreed Outcome
    Book the BBA within 48 hours. Choose your approach based on customer intent:
    Low Intent
    "If you wish to proceed with XYZ Bank, please upload your documents before our next appointment so I can review servicing and prepare the application."
    High Intent
    "We actually offer same-day submission within 24 hours — could you jump on a quick video call now so we can finalise the details and get your application underway today?"
Common objections at close
"I need to talk to my partner first"
Your response Completely understand — we'd need to sit down together for the video call anyway, so this is just about setting up what next steps would look like for you both. Does XYZ seem like the lender you're leaning towards right now?
"Let me think about it and I'll get back to you"
Your response No problem — we aren't looking to proceed right now anyway since I'll need your documents, so you'll have time to think. Just so I can show you what those documents look like: does one of the lenders we spoke about stand out more than the other?
"I don't think I'll do anything right now"
Your response No stress — I know that's a lot of information to take in. Was there anything specific holding you back? I can see we could definitely move forward quickly with the refi or AIP for you.
"Can we push it back a month?"
Your response Completely understand — that's where we help. We manage the whole process for you from start to finish; we could even have it approved and settled within a month. All you'd need to do right now is upload your ID and pay slips. Would you be free now — or this afternoon — for a quick video call?
"I'm going to wait and save up more"
Your response I understand — timing can matter. But what's actually holding you back? I've run the numbers and can see you'd be able to enter the market now. We could get you a pre-approval in your back pocket for when you're ready to make an offer.
🏠

Step 05
Follow Up Process
Key rules
  • Every follow-up call must have a purpose — re-sell the outcome and link back to why the customer came to you.
  • Book the BBA within 48 hours. The further out the appointment, the more customer intent drops off.
  • Make a minimum of 3 contact attempts, each 1–2 days apart.
3-Attempt Contact Strategy
  • Attempt 1
    Call at the time of the BBA
    If no answer, leave a voicemail.
  • Attempt 2
    Call again 1–2 days later
    If no answer, leave a voicemail and send an email reminder.
  • Attempt 3
    Final attempt
    If no answer, leave a voicemail advising you will now close the file. Let them know they can reach back out by phone or email when they're ready to proceed.
Handling follow-up friction
📁 Documents not yet uploaded
Your response I can see there are only a couple of items left. Let me re-send you the link now — can you upload while we're on the phone so we can get it out of the way? We want to get your application to the bank ASAP to avoid the queue and lock in your approval.
🤔 Customer still hasn't decided
Your response As we've discussed, there are definite savings on the table. Which part of the process or which lender is holding you back? [If unresolved after 3 attempts → close lost.]
⏰
Build urgency with a deadline: "I don't want to waste your time with follow-up calls, and I know you don't want to waste mine. If I haven't received your documents by [date], I'll close off your file — you can always reach back out when you have more time."
